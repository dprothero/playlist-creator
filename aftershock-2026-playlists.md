# Aftershock 2026 — Listening Playlists

## Playlist 1: Priority Acts

These are the closest matches to melodic death metal, metalcore, and the heavier side of your taste.

---

### Killswitch Engage
*Melodic metalcore — the genre's gold standard. If you dig Architects and Parkway Drive, this is home turf.*
- **The End of Heartache** — Their signature track. Melodic metalcore perfection.
- **My Curse** — Huge chorus, iconic riff.
- **Rose of Sharyn** — Another essential. Clean/harsh dynamic at its best.
- **In Due Time** — More accessible but still hits hard.

### The Black Dahlia Murder
*Melodic death metal — the most direct hit for your In Flames side.*
- **Funeral Thirst** — Classic TBDM. Relentless melodeath.
- **What a Horrible Night to Have a Curse** — One of their most well-known tracks.
- **Nightbringers** — Title track from a strong later album. Fast and vicious.
- **Verminous** — Great showcase of their twin-guitar melodeath attack.

### Blue Medusa (Alissa White-Gluz)
*Brand new project — debut single dropped March 20, 2026. Melodic death metal with a broader vocal range. For context, here's her Arch Enemy work too.*
- **Checkmate** (Blue Medusa) — The debut single. Melodic hooks meets death metal riffing.
- **The Room Where She Died** (solo single) — Released Nov 2025 upon her Arch Enemy exit.
- **War Eternal** (Arch Enemy) — Her Arch Enemy debut. Sets the tone immediately.
- **The Eagle Flies Alone** (Arch Enemy) — Catchy, anthemic melodeath.

### After The Burial
*Progressive metalcore/djent — technical, heavy, groove-driven.*
- **A Wolf Amongst Ravens** — Probably their most popular track. Crushing.
- **Behold the Crown** — Heavy and groovy with strong melodic sections.
- **Lost in the Static** — Djenty riffs with melodic leads.
- **Collapse** — Aggressive and dynamic.

### The Devil Wears Prada
*Metalcore — aggressive with atmospheric and melodic elements.*
- **Danger: Wildman** — Fan favorite. Peak early metalcore energy.
- **Chemical** — From their later, more refined era.
- **Sacrifice** — Mature metalcore with big hooks.
- **Watchtower** — Atmospheric and heavy.

### Wage War
*Modern metalcore — straightforward, heavy, accessible.*
- **Stitch** — Their breakout track. Simple and effective.
- **Low** — Heavier, more aggressive.
- **Manic** — Good mix of clean and harsh.
- **Grave** — Crushing low-end, solid riffs.

### The Ghost Inside
*Melodic hardcore/metalcore — positive lyrics, heavy delivery.*
- **Aftermath** — Comeback track after their devastating bus crash. Powerful.
- **Engine 45** — One of their most well-known songs.
- **Avalanche** — Anthemic and driving.
- **Move Me** — Energetic and catchy.

### Counterparts
*Melodic hardcore — emotional, dark, and heavy.*
- **The Disconnect** — Excellent intro to their sound.
- **Paradise and Plague** — Dark and melodic with crushing breakdowns.
- **Wings of Nightmares** — Aggressive with hooks.
- **Bound to the Burn** — Raw and intense.

## Playlist 2: Worth Checking Out

Broader picks — different flavors of heavy that could land depending on mood.

---

### Cavalera (performing Sepultura's *Roots*)
*Groove/thrash metal — Max and Iggor Cavalera playing Roots front to back. Iconic heavy music.*
- **Roots Bloody Roots** — One of the most recognizable metal riffs ever written.
- **Attitude** — Aggressive groove metal.
- **Ratamahatta** — Tribal percussion meets thrash. Unique.
- **Cut-Throat** — Pure aggression.

### Tool
*Progressive metal — complex, hypnotic, cerebral. Best live production on the bill.*
- **Schism** — Their most well-known track. Shifting time signatures.
- **Forty Six & 2** — Heavy, progressive, and memorable.
- **Lateralus** — The magnum opus. Fibonacci spiral structure.
- **Fear Inoculum** — Title track from their 2019 album. Long-form prog metal.

### Slaughter To Prevail
*Deathcore — Russian brutality. Heavier and more guttural than your typical lane.*
- **Bonebreaker** — Crushing. Alex Terrible's vocals are inhuman.
- **Demolisher** — Probably their most viral track.
- **Viking** — Melodic elements creep in here.
- **Agony** — Relentless heaviness.

### Rivers of Nihil
*Progressive death metal — atmospheric, saxophone-infused, technical.*
- **Where Owls Know My Name** — The standout. Sax solo in a death metal song. It works.
- **The Silent Life** — Clean, atmospheric, then crushingly heavy.
- **Clean** — Beautiful and haunting.
- **Focus** — More traditional prog-death aggression.

### Alexisonfire
*Post-hardcore — melodic, emotional, heavy when it needs to be.*
- **This Could Be Anywhere in the World** — Their biggest track.
- **Pulmonary Archery** — Raw, energetic, dual-vocal attack.
- **Young Cardinals** — Polished but still has edge.
- **Boiled Frogs** — Great songwriting, builds beautifully.

### Underoath
*Post-hardcore/metalcore — chaotic, atmospheric, emotional.*
- **Writing on the Walls** — Their most recognized song. Intense and catchy.
- **It's Dangerous Business Walking Out Your Front Door** — Early era classic.
- **A Boy Brushed Red Living in Black and White** — Iconic mid-2000s metalcore.
- **Reinventing Your Exit** — Driving and urgent.

### Atreyu
*Melodic metalcore — especially their earlier material leans hard into the genre.*
- **Lip Gloss and Black** — Classic. Melodeath-influenced metalcore.
- **The Crimson** — Anthemic, big hooks.
- **Ex's and Oh's** — Aggressive and catchy.
- **Right Side of the Bed** — Solid melodic metalcore.

### Cradle of Filth
*Symphonic/extreme metal — theatrical, fast, orchestral. A wildcard.*
- **Her Ghost in the Fog** — Probably their most acclaimed track.
- **Nymphetamine Fix** — Most accessible entry point. Gothic and melodic.
- **From the Cradle to Enslave** — Fast, aggressive, symphonic.
- **Heartbreak and Séance** — Good later-era track.

### DOWN
*Sludge/doom metal — Phil Anselmo's post-Pantera project. Low, slow, heavy.*
- **Stone the Crow** — The essential DOWN track. Massive riff.
- **Lifer** — Sludgy and groovy.
- **Bury Me in Smoke** — Doom-laden and crushing.
- **Ghosts Along the Mississippi** — Atmospheric Southern doom.
